AI Engine

The intelligence layer powering every dispatch, review, and prediction on SHARE-RIDE.

What is the AI Engine?

The SHARE-RIDE AI Engine is a collection of machine learning models and groq-ai integrations that work together behind the scenes to make smarter decisions than any rule-based system could achieve alone.

It handles three core jobs: matching the right driver to every ride, analyzing rider and driver feedback with natural language understanding, and predicting demand before it happens.

Driver-Rider Matching Process

Each ride request triggers a real-time scoring process. Drivers are ranked, notified, and assigned based on multiple weighted metrics.

Proximity to Rider 40%

System queries drivers within a 5 km radius. Closest drivers earn higher proximity scores using Haversine formula and live traffic adjustments.

Driver Status 15%

Only drivers who are ONLINE are eligible. Offline drivers are filtered out automatically.

Driver Rating 25%

Rolling average of the last 50 completed rides, weighted for recency. Includes AI sentiment analysis from rider feedback.

Response Time 10%

Measures how quickly a driver accepts or declines ride requests. Faster responders have higher scores.

Ride History & Experience 10%

Considers number of completed rides, cancellations, and experience in the pickup zone over the last 30 days.

Formula: Score = (0.40 × Proximity) + (0.15 × Online Status) + (0.25 × Rating) + (0.10 × Response) + (0.10 × History)

Dispatch — Step by Step

1
Rider Submits Request

Pickup & drop coordinates are sent to the backend via REST API. The ride document is created with status requested.

2
Nearby Drivers Queried

MongoDB geospatial index returns all available drivers within a configurable radius (default 5km). Redis caches the current positions for speed.

3
AI Scores Each Driver

The scoring engine computes a weighted score for every candidate using the four metrics above. Scores are logged for transparency and future RL training.

4
Best Match Notified

The top-scoring driver receives a push notification (FCM) and a Socket.IO event with ride details. They have 30 seconds to accept or reject.

5
Fallback Cascade

If rejected or timed out, the next-best driver is tried automatically. Stale requests older than 5 minutes are auto-cancelled with the rider notified.

How It Works

Rider submits a text review via the app after ride completion.

Review is sent to POST /api/ai/analyze-review — our node-js groq-AI endpoint.

groq-AI extracts sentiment, scores the review 1–10, and categorizes the feedback.

Actionable tips are generated and stored in the Feedback collection.

Driver dashboard surfaces the tips. Aggregate scores feed into the dispatch rating metric.
